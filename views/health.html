Nutrition Tips

  • Keep healthy snacks like fruits, nuts, and granola bars handy.

  • Choose balanced meals with protein, whole grains, and vegetables at the cafeteria.

  • Prep meals on weekends for easy access during the week.

  • Carry a water bottle and drink at least 8 glasses of water daily.

  • Avoid late-night snacking to maintain healthy digestion.



    Sleep Tips

  • Set a consistent sleep schedule, even on weekends.

  • Create a dark, quiet, and cool sleep environment.

  • Avoid caffeine in the afternoon to ensure better sleep at night.

  • Develop a relaxing bedtime routine to signal to your body it’s time to sleep.

  • Reduce screen time before bed to improve sleep quality.



    Exercise Tips

  • Always warm up before your workout to prevent injury.

  • Combine cardio and strength training for a balanced routine.

  • Set realistic goals and gradually increase intensity.

  • Stay consistent with at least 3-4 workouts per week.

  • Hydrate well before, during, and after exercise.


Nutrition Tasks

  • Stay within your daily calorie goal (e.g., 2,000-2,500 calories) by tracking meals using a calorie-counting app.

  • Eat balanced meals with 400-600 calories each to maintain energy throughout the day without overeating.

  • Aim for 300-400 calorie snacks to avoid overeating later while keeping your metabolism active.

  • Avoid skipping meals to ensure you hit your calorie target and maintain steady energy levels.

  • Set a calorie target for each meal: for example, breakfast at 300-400 calories, lunch at 500-600 calories, and dinner at 500-600 calories.



Sleep Tasks

  • Set a consistent sleep schedule by going to bed and waking up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

  • Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep each night to ensure you’re well-rested for classes and activities.

  • Take short naps (20-30 minutes) during the day if you need to recharge without disrupting your nighttime sleep.

  • Avoid caffeine after 2 PM to help fall asleep more easily at night.

  • Establish a bedtime routine (like reading or meditation) to signal to your body that it's time to wind down.



Exercise Tasks

  • Strength train at least 3 times a week focusing on different muscle groups each session (e.g., legs, back, chest).

  • Run or bike for 20-30 minutes on cardio machines (treadmill, stationary bike) at least 3 times a week for cardiovascular health.

  • Increase your bench press by 5-10 pounds every month to build upper body strength.

  • Do 3 sets of 10-15 reps for each major muscle group (e.g., squats, deadlifts, pull-ups) to improve overall strength.

  • Incorporate HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) workouts twice a week to improve endurance and burn fat.
